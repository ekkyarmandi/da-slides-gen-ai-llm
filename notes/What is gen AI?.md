### Key Drivers:

<ul id="key-drivers" style="line-height: 2;">
  <li class="fragment" data-fragment-index="1">Creative automation</li>
  <li class="fragment" data-fragment-index="2">Natural interaction</li>
  <li class="fragment" data-fragment-index="3">Cost reduction</li>
  <li class="fragment" data-fragment-index="4">Rapid prototyping</li>
  <li class="fragment" data-fragment-index="5">Cross-industry applicability</li>
</ul>

note:
- **Creative automation** → Generates content at scale (text, code, images) that previously required human expertise
- **Natural interaction** → Conversational interfaces make AI accessible to non-technical users
- **Cost reduction** → Automates knowledge work, reducing time from hours to seconds
- **Rapid prototyping** → Enables quick iteration on ideas without specialized skills
- **Cross-industry applicability** → Transforms everything from customer service to drug discovery

Source: [OpenAI Research](https://chatgpt.com/s/t_686512df670481918f69e44ed0b3772c)

note:
**Interpretation of the chart**
Explosive take-off (2023 → 2024) – Within a single year, the share of Indonesians exposed to AI tools doubled, driven by mobile-first generative-AI apps, Bahasa-Indonesia interfaces, and viral usage of ChatGPT and image generators.

**Underlying drivers**

- Mobile affordability
- Local-language UX
- Platform push – e-commerce, banking and ride-hailing apps embedded AI helpers (copywriting, fraud alerts, dynamic pricing), nudging millions of users into first-time contact with AI.
- Outlook – With 8 in 10 Indonesians already acquainted with AI, the next frontier is depth of use: moving from occasional chat and translation into embedded, task-specific agents (e.g., tax filing, farm-price forecasting) and ensuring trust and digital-skills keep pace with penetration.
